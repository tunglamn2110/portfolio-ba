Metiseko Customer Behavior Analysis

Applying S-O-R framework and Python clustering to decode customer psychology and drive loyalty strategies.

Project Overview

METISEKO is a sustainable fashion brand looking to understand its e-commerce user base better. As a Data-Driven Business Analyst, I conducted a quantitative study using the Stimulus-Organism-Response (S-O-R) framework. The goal was to identify key psychological drivers of customer loyalty and segment users based on their behavioral patterns to propose targeted UX/UI improvements.

The Problem

  • Unidentified Loyalty Drivers: The brand lacked clarity on whether customers were driven by Visuals, Convenience, or Trust.
  • One-size-fits-all Strategy: Marketing and UX strategies were generic, failing to address specific needs of different user groups.
  • Complex Behavioral Data: Raw survey data with 25 different variables was unstructured and difficult to translate into actionable business insights.

The Process (Data Analysis)

1. Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA)

Used Python (FactorAnalyzer) to reduce dimensions. Validated 5 core constructs from 25 variables: Visual Appeal, Informativeness, Interactive Experience, Self-Control, and Affective Ease (Trust + Convenience).

2. K-Means Clustering

Applied K-Means algorithm to segment customers based on psychometric scores. Determined the optimal number of clusters (k=3) using the Elbow Method.

3. Visualization & Strategy

Created Snake Plots (Z-Score analysis) to visualize persona differences. Developed specific UX recommendations for each segment (e.g., improving navigation for "Aesthetic Explorers").

Identified Personas:

  • 1. Pragmatic Regulars: Loyal users who value navigation efficiency over visuals.
  • 2. Selective Comfort Seekers: Demanding users seeking trust & information, hard to please.
  • 3. Aesthetic-Driven Explorers: Users attracted by visuals/interaction but struggle with UX.
